Body by Science Home Workout

A simple dumbbell version for home: move slowly, keep tension on the muscle, and stop the set when form breaks.

Squat Press Row Chest RDL Progress

How to run the session

Body by Science style: this is intended as a brief, hard, controlled full-body workout. Use a load that lets Shmee work continuously and safely for roughly 60–90 seconds per exercise.

TempoSlow reps. Aim for about 5 seconds up and 5 seconds down. No bouncing or locking out to rest.
EffortFor Shmee’s first few sessions, stop with 1–2 good reps left. Later, stop when she cannot keep clean form.
RestRest 60–120 seconds between exercises, or longer if needed to set up safely.
FrequencyUsually once weekly for the hard version. Add a second lighter strength day later only if running recovery is good.
Safety: warm up gently, avoid sharp pain, and keep the movements controlled. For Shmee’s marathon training, this should support running—not wreck the legs for days. Increase weight only after two workouts in a row above ~90 seconds with solid form and no unusual soreness, pelvic heaviness/pressure, back pain, Achilles pain, or knee complaints.
Breathing rule: breathe in during the easier/lowering phase, then breathe out during the effort phase. She can gently brace before the hard part, but she should not hold her breath through the whole slow rep.

1. Goblet Squat

Lower body push

Naomi goblet squat demo.

Open YouTube
Suggested start25 lb
Starting range20–30 lb
Target1 set · 60–90 sec · ~6–9 slow reps

Form and breathing

  • Setup: hold one dumbbell vertically at chest height, elbows tucked slightly down, feet about shoulder-width, toes gently turned out.
  • Lower: breathe in as she slowly sits between her heels. Keep chest proud, ribs stacked over hips, and knees tracking in the same direction as toes.
  • Push: breathe out as she pushes the floor away and stands up. Think “drive through mid-foot and heel,” not “tip forward onto toes.”
  • Top: stop just short of relaxing. Stand tall, squeeze glutes lightly, then start the next slow descent without resting.
  • Watch for: knees collapsing inward, heels lifting, rounding the lower back, or turning this into fast reps.

Body by Science cue

Keep continuous tension. The set should feel like one long controlled effort, not separate reps with rests at the top.

2. Dumbbell Overhead Press

Upper body push

Heather Robertson overhead press demo.

Open YouTube
Suggested start10 lb each
Starting range8–12 lb each
Target1 set · 60–90 sec · ~6–9 slow reps

Form and breathing

  • Setup: stand tall or sit on a sturdy chair. Start dumbbells near shoulder height, palms facing forward or slightly inward.
  • Brace: glutes lightly on, ribs down, belly gently braced. Do not lean back to turn it into an incline press.
  • Push: breathe out as she presses the dumbbells overhead. Move slowly and keep wrists stacked over elbows.
  • Lower: breathe in as she lowers the dumbbells back toward shoulder height under control.
  • Watch for: arching the lower back, shrugging into the ears, flaring ribs, or locking out and resting at the top.

Body by Science cue

Keep the shoulders working the whole time. Smooth reps beat heavy sloppy reps, especially on this lift.

3. Dumbbell Bent-Over Row

Upper body pull

Colossus Fit bent-over row demo.

Open YouTube
Suggested start15 lb each
Starting range12–20 lb each
Target1 set · 60–90 sec · ~6–9 slow reps

Form and breathing

  • Setup: hinge at the hips with soft knees, long spine, and chest angled toward the floor. Dumbbells hang under shoulders.
  • Pull: breathe out as she rows the dumbbells toward her lower ribs/hips. Lead with elbows, not hands.
  • Lower: breathe in as she lowers the dumbbells slowly until arms are long again without letting shoulders dump forward.
  • Back position: keep neck neutral, shoulder blades moving smoothly, and torso still.
  • Watch for: yanking with momentum, rounding the back, shrugging, or standing up during the rep.

Body by Science cue

The torso stays quiet. If she needs body English or momentum, the set is over.

4. Dumbbell Floor Press

Chest press, no bench
No bench adaptation: use a dumbbell floor press instead of a dumbbell bench press. The floor limits depth and is usually safer at home.

Colossus Fit dumbbell floor press demo.

Open YouTube
Suggested start15 lb each
Starting range12–20 lb each
Target1 set · 60–90 sec · ~6–9 slow reps

Form and breathing

  • Setup: lie on the floor with knees bent and feet planted. Start with dumbbells over chest, wrists straight, elbows about 30–45° from the body.
  • Lower: breathe in as she lowers slowly until upper arms lightly meet the floor. Do not bounce the elbows.
  • Push: breathe out as she presses the dumbbells up. Think “push away from the floor” while keeping shoulder blades gently set.
  • Top: stop just short of hard elbow lockout so the chest, shoulders, and triceps stay under tension.
  • Watch for: wrists bending back, elbows flaring straight out, shrugging shoulders, or bouncing off the floor.

Body by Science cue

Keep tension across chest, shoulders, and triceps. The floor is a safety stop, not a trampoline.

5. Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift

Hip hinge

NASM dumbbell Romanian deadlift demo.

Open YouTube
Suggested start20 lb each
Starting range15–25 lb each
Target1 set · 60–90 sec · ~6–9 slow reps

Form and breathing

  • Setup: hold two dumbbells in front of thighs, feet hip-width, knees soft, shoulders relaxed.
  • Lower: breathe in as she sends hips back and lets the dumbbells slide down close to the legs. Spine stays long and ribs stay down.
  • Depth: stop when hamstrings are loaded or when the back wants to round. She does not need to reach the floor.
  • Stand: breathe out as she drives hips forward and returns to tall standing. Think “glutes forward,” not “pull with the low back.”
  • Watch for: squatting too much, dumbbells drifting away from legs, rounding the back, or locking knees hard.

Body by Science cue

This is not a squat. Make it a slow hip hinge and keep the dumbbells close to the legs the whole time.

When to change the weight

Under 45–60 secToo heavy for now, or tempo/form is breaking. Keep the same weight or go lighter.
60–90 secPerfect working zone. Keep the weight and try to make the set smoother next time.
Over 90 sec twiceMove up next session if recovery is good and there are no pain or postpartum pressure symptoms.
Over 120 secDefinitely increase weight next time.
Multiple sets: keep this as one work set per exercise. Add only light practice reps before the work set if she is learning the movement or misjudged the first weight.
